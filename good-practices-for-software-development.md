# Good Practices for Software Development – Review

## Question 1  
### Which point(s) were new to you?

Some points that were new or more clearly explained to me were:

- The idea of **over-communicating**. I used to think communicating only when needed is enough, but now I understand that in remote work it is better to inform the team even about small delays or issues.

- The proper way to **ask questions when stuck**. Instead of just saying "it is not working", we should explain the problem clearly, mention what we tried, and share screenshots or error logs. This makes it easier for others to help.

- Using **group channels instead of private messages** most of the time. This helps everyone stay aware of what is happening and avoids confusion.

- The importance of **deep focus while working**. I didn’t think much about attention management before, but now I understand that programming needs strong concentration.

## Question 2  
### Which area do you think you need to improve on? What are your ideas to make progress in that area?

I think I need to improve mainly in **communication and focus**.

### 1. Improving Communication

Sometimes I try to solve issues alone for too long before asking for help. I should:

- Ask questions earlier if I am stuck
- Clearly explain the problem instead of sending short messages
- Inform the team if something is taking longer than expected
- Write down requirements clearly and confirm them with the team

### 2. Improving Focus During Work

I also need to work on my concentration. To improve this, I will:

- Keep my phone on silent during work hours
- Avoid social media while working
- Work in focused time blocks
- Take short breaks to maintain energy
