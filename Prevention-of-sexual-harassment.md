# Prevention of Sexual Harassment (PoSH)

## 1. What kinds of behaviour cause sexual harassment?

Sexual harassment refers to any **unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature** that directly or indirectly affects an individual’s dignity at the workplace or creates an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating, or offensive environment.

Such behaviour may be **physical, verbal, non-verbal, written, visual, or digital**.

### Forms of Sexual Harassment

#### 1. Physical Conduct
- Unwelcome physical contact or advances  
- Touching, grabbing, brushing intentionally  
- Attempted or actual assault  

#### 2. Verbal Conduct
- Sexually coloured remarks or jokes  
- Comments about someone’s appearance, body, or personal life  
- Repeated unwanted invitations for dates  
- Requests or demands for sexual favours  

#### 3. Non-Verbal Conduct
- Staring, leering, or suggestive gestures  
- Displaying sexually explicit material  
- Sharing inappropriate images or videos  

#### 4. Quid Pro Quo Harassment
- Implicit or explicit promise of professional benefits in exchange for sexual favours  
- Threats of negative consequences (poor appraisal, termination, exclusion) for refusal  

#### 5. Creating a Hostile Work Environment
- Repeated inappropriate behaviour causing discomfort  
- Bullying or humiliation with sexual undertones  
- Any conduct that interferes with work performance or mental well-being  

Even if the behaviour is presented as a “joke,” it constitutes harassment if it is **unwelcome and causes discomfort**.

## 2. What would you do in case you face or witness any incident or repeated incidents of such behaviour?

Maintaining a safe and respectful workplace is a collective responsibility. If I face or witness such behaviour, I would take the following steps:

### If I Face Sexual Harassment

1. **Communicate Clearly (if safe to do so)**  
   Inform the person that the behaviour is unwelcome and inappropriate.

2. **Document the Incident**  
   Record details such as:
   - Date and time  
   - Location  
   - Description of the incident  
   - Names of witnesses (if any)

3. **Preserve Evidence**  
   Save emails, messages, screenshots, or any other relevant proof.

4. **Report to the Appropriate Authority**  
   Submit a complaint to:
   - The Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)  
   - HR or designated PoSH authority  

5. **Cooperate with the Inquiry Process**  
   Maintain confidentiality and participate honestly in the investigation.

---

### If I Witness Sexual Harassment

1. **Support the Affected Individual**  
   Encourage them to report the incident.

2. **Report the Behaviour (if appropriate)**  
   Inform HR or the Internal Complaints Committee.

3. **Do Not Ignore or Normalize Inappropriate Conduct**  
   Silence enables misconduct.

4. **Promote Respectful Workplace Culture**  
   Lead by example and uphold professional boundaries.
