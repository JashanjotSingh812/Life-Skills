# Listening and Active Communication

---

## 1. Active Listening

### Question 1  
**What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?**

1. Avoid getting distracted by your own thoughts. Focus instead on the speaker and topic.
2. Try not to interrupt the other person. Let them finish and then respond.
3. Use door openers. These are phrases that show you are interested and keep the other person talking.
4. Show that you are listening with body language.
5. If appropriate, take notes during important conversations.
6. Paraphrase what others have said to make sure you are on the same page.


---

## 2. Reflective Listening

### Question 2  
**According to Fisher’s model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?**

1. Carefully listen to both facts and emotions.
2. Reflect or restate the message in your own words.
3. Acknowledge the speaker’s feelings.
4. Verify whether your understanding is correct.
5. Avoid judgment, advice, or assumptions.
6. Allow the speaker to correct or clarify if needed.

---

## 3. Reflection

### Question 3  
**What are the obstacles in your listening process?**

1. Distractions like mobile phones or notifications.
2. Thinking about what to reply instead of listening.
3. Assuming the message before the speaker finishes.
4. Emotional reactions such as stress or frustration.
5. Lack of focus due to tiredness.
6. Difficulty understanding complex or technical details.

---

### Question 4  
**What can you do to improve your listening?**

1. Keep distractions away during conversations.
2. Practice patience and avoid interruptions.
3. Take notes during important discussions.
4. Ask questions instead of making assumptions.
5. Pay attention to emotions and tone.
6. Summarize the discussion to ensure clarity.

## 4. Types of Communication

### Question 5  
**When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?**

1. While talking to seniors or authority figures.
2. When I want to avoid conflict.
3. When I lack confidence in my opinion.
4. In situations where I feel uncomfortable speaking up.
5. During group discussions dominated by others.

---

### Question 6  
**When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?**

1. When I feel repeatedly misunderstood.
2. During high-stress or pressure situations.
3. When my boundaries are ignored.
4. In heated arguments.
5. When emotions overpower logical thinking.

---

### Question 7  
**When do you switch into Passive-Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?**

1. When I am upset but avoid confrontation.
2. When I feel ignored or unappreciated.
3. Using sarcasm instead of direct communication.
4. Giving silent treatment instead of expressing feelings.
5. Complaining to others rather than addressing the issue directly.

---

## 5. Assertive Communication

### Question 8  
**How can you make your communication assertive?**

1. Express thoughts clearly and respectfully.
2. Use “I” statements instead of blaming language.
3. Maintain a calm tone and positive body language.
4. Listen actively before responding.
5. Set boundaries politely and confidently.
6. Avoid sarcasm, shouting, or passive behavior.
7. Be confident without being dominant.
8. Practice honesty with empathy.
