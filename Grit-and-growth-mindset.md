## 2. Introduction to Growth Mindset

### Question 2  
**Paraphrase (summarize) the video in a few (1 or 2) lines in your own words.**

A growth mindset is the belief that intelligence and abilities can be developed through effort, learning, and persistence. People with this mindset see challenges and failures as opportunities to grow rather than as limits.

---

## 3. Understanding Internal Locus of Control

### Question 3  
**What is the Internal Locus of Control? What is the key point in the video?**

Internal Locus of Control is the belief that your actions, decisions, and efforts determine your results, instead of external factors like luck or circumstances.

The key point of the video is to focus only on what you can control—your attitude, effort, and behavior. Taking responsibility increases motivation and personal growth.

---

## 4. How to Build a Growth Mindset

### Question 4  
**What are the key points mentioned by the speaker to build a growth mindset?**

- Accept challenges  
- Learn from failures  
- Replace negative self-talk with positive learning statements  
- Value effort over talent  
- Take feedback positively  
- Practice consistently  
- Be patient with progress  
- Keep improving daily

## 5. My Action Plan to Build a Growth Mindset

### Question 5  
**What are your ideas to take action and build a Growth Mindset?**

As a software developer, I will take the following actions:

- I will treat bugs and errors as learning opportunities instead of getting frustrated.
- I will practice coding daily to improve my problem-solving ability.
- I will ask questions when I don’t understand something.
- I will accept feedback from mentors and improve accordingly.
- I will focus on consistency rather than quick success.
- I will attempt challenging problems even if I fail at first.
- I will reflect weekly on my learning and identify areas to improve.
- I will avoid comparing myself to others and focus on my own progress.
- I will remind myself that skills grow with effort and time.
